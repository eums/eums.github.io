<h1 style="text-align: justify;">EUMS Chorus<&#47;h1><br />
<img alt="" src="http:&#47;&#47;eums.eusa.ed.ac.uk&#47;wp-content&#47;uploads&#47;images&#47;w620&#47;chorus07.jpg" width="620" height="250" &#47;></p>
<p><strong>The Edinburgh University Music Society Chorus is one of the largest choirs in Scotland,&nbsp;performing several times a year in front of large audiences. EUMS Chorus is a non-auditioning SATB symphonic chorus and we welcome both students and non-students asmembers. Rehearsals take place every Tuesday from 1830-2045 in Room G1, Paterson&rsquo;s&nbsp;Land, Holyrood Road during the&nbsp;University&nbsp;of Edinburgh&rsquo;s term-time.<&#47;strong></p>
<blockquote><p><em><span style="color: #ff0000;">&ldquo;The intelligence of the chorus of University students, and their&nbsp;evident determination to do&nbsp;their best before an Edinburgh audience,&nbsp;made&nbsp;their conductor feel proud of them.&rdquo;<&#47;span><&#47;em></p>
<p><a title="Past Conductors" href="http:&#47;&#47;eums.eusa.ed.ac.uk&#47;society&#47;conductors&#47;past-conductors&#47;">Sir Herbert Oakeley, EUMS founder<&#47;a><&#47;blockquote></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">As with <a title="Biography" href="http:&#47;&#47;eums.eusa.ed.ac.uk&#47;society&#47;biography&#47;">the other ensembles<&#47;a> of EUMS,&nbsp;Chorus puts on a concert at the end of each semester in various&nbsp;venues including St. Cuthbert&rsquo;s Church, Greyfriars Kirk and St. Mary&rsquo;s Episcopal Cathedral. Though&nbsp;the Chorus is non-auditioning, the quality of our performances is very high, and with the numbers&nbsp;behind us we produce a fantastic sound.&nbsp;Don't take our word for it, make up your own mind: here is an excerpt from a recent recording of us performing Rossini's <em>Petite Messe Solennelle<&#47;em>.<&#47;p><br />
[audio:http:&#47;&#47;eums.eusa.ed.ac.uk&#47;wp-content&#47;uploads&#47;audio&#47;snippets&#47;chorus_rossini_30s.mp3]</p>
<p>Our Chorus performs challenging pieces of music from a wide classical repertoire, with recent&nbsp;works including Bernstein&rsquo;s Chichester Psalms, Jenkins&rsquo;&nbsp;The Armed Man&nbsp;and Verdi&rsquo;s&nbsp;Messa&nbsp;de&nbsp;Requiem.&nbsp;Chorus also collaborates with one of our orchestras once a year to perform large choral&nbsp;works, which have recently included Mendelssohn&rsquo;s Symphony No.2 &ldquo;Hymn of Praise and the 2011&nbsp;sell-out concert Elgar&rsquo;s Dream of Gerontius.</p>
<p>[caption id="" align="aligncenter" width="405"]<a href="http:&#47;&#47;eums.eusa.ed.ac.uk&#47;wp-content&#47;uploads&#47;2011&#47;03&#47;chorus.jpg"><img class=" wp-image-3067 " style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" alt="chorus" src="http:&#47;&#47;eums.eusa.ed.ac.uk&#47;wp-content&#47;uploads&#47;2011&#47;03&#47;chorus.jpg" width="405" height="270" &#47;><&#47;a> Chorus perform Poulenc's&nbsp;<em>Gloria<&#47;em>[&#47;caption]</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p style="text-align: center;">Though there is no requirement to read music, it can certainly help, but whether you are a mini-maestro or indeed a hairbrush-singer, then there is certainly a place for you.<&#47;p><br />
